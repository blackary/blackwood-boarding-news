## One Itty-Bitty Birthday Cake

Student Emory Blackwood enjoyed celebrating her 10th birthday this week (and especially opening presents!) She received a book and a party game, but the highlight gift was a teeny tiny kitchen set. The first thing she concocted was her first-ever cake. She mixed together pinches of this and drops of that into the miniature bowls with teensy weensy spatulas and tablespoons, and plopped the batter into the mini pans. Dawning her finger-sized oven mitts, Emory set them in the very large oven. Then she iced the very small chocolate cake, and sliced it into tiny nibbles for everyone to sample, that is, if they could taste something so itty-bitty.

## A Fabulous Weekend Retreat

The RGC youth group had an amazing getaway this weekend (thanks to the pancake breakfast back in early April!), and especially enjoyed hearing the honorable guest speaker Zachary Blackwood, who talked about the book of Revelation. The group enjoyed fishing, party games, junk food, and chatting late into the night. The few days went successfully without any fatalities and the leaders did a wonderful job making it such a fun time. 

## Construction Wrapping Up

The work on Blackwood Manor is finally nearing completion! Tools and machines are moving out, and interior decorations are moving in! Some students enjoyed ripping up the brown paper covering the floors in the dining room (it just wasn’t bringing the space together as well as the builders had expected.) Light fixtures and appliances are being brought in, and the whole house is coming together and finally starting to look like a real living space. Come drop by 544 Angelic Lane and see it for yourself!
