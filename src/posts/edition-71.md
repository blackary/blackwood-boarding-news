## Going Bananas at Family Camp

This week BBSG experienced the successful, entertaining, memory-making, and sleep-depriving Redeeming Grace Family Camp! Principal Zachary Blackwood was unable to attend the first half of the retreat while he was at his conference in San Francisco, but he did enjoy the time he was able to be there. Afterwards he remarked, “Camp was great, but beating Samuel and Titus at Unfathomable was definitely the highlight.” While the principal competed in six hour board games, Blackwood Boarding students and teachers passed the time playing 9 Square, swimming, paddle-boarding, rockclimbing, and participating in group activities like RGC Baby Faces and the greased watermelon contest! BBSG gives much thanks to all those who made this amazing time possible! 

## Heavens Open at The Manor

Just after a sunny and warm Family Camp, we returned home to a complete downpour at Blackwood Manor. Thick sheets of rain crashed down, flooding the driveway, yard, and of course Lake Felicity. Thunder exploded in the distance, and streaks of lightning occasionally flashed. Storms like these were reported across town, all very small, but very intense. The weather continued looping in a cycle of going from hot humid and sunny to pounding rain within less than an hour. Many Blackwood Boarding girls actually enjoyed getting completely soaked, especially when BBSG attended an outdoor graduation party in the park (thankfully it was under a shelter) and rivers and lakes formed all over the playground. Any visitors to Blackwood Manor, look out for plenty of mud this coming week!

## Officially Moving In

The very very very long awaited move-in to Blackwood Manor has finally come, and BBSG has officially relocated to our new school building! There is still plenty of work to be done on the house, as well as unpacking and organizing our belongings, but now that we have made the move things will hopefully move along quickly and smoothly. While Flo B. is excited to be fully moved in, it can be stressful unpacking, cleaning, and finding a place for essential belongings. Thankfully the house is now less of a wreck and things are coming together, making a more accommodating space.
