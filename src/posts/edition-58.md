## Springtime on It’s Way

Soon after returning from our world travels, Blackwood Boarding Girls have said goodbye to frigid winter, and we’re getting used to warmer weather…again! Of course along with springtime comes April showers, which have been premiering in Indiana, and yardwork! BBSG’s yard has been in–well–not the best shape over the past few months, so there has been plenty to get done. We spent most of Saturday morning weeding, digging, raking, and picking up pinecones and sticks, and the school grounds are starting to look more orderly. Next comes tidying up Blackwood Manor. 

## RGC’s Got Talent

Who knew?! This week was an exciting and hilarious church talent night. There were certainly some award winning acts, with instrument playing, singing, dancing, and more! It was a five-star show that entertained folks of all ages in the audience. Zachary Blackwood remarked, “It contained the most impressive display of nasal musical ability that I’ve ever seen.” Yes, you read that correctly. 

## The Newest Class Pet

This week Allison Blackwood discovered an enormous crawdad crawling under the car, and quickly put it in a bucket to show off to the rest of the school. The creature was about the size of…a really big crawdad! Later that day, Flo Blackwood shrieked, and threw her slipper at a spider on the floor. It later became Mr. Crawdad’s lunch, along with a fresh worm. For some reason, the bucket containing the crustacean was allowed to stay inside where Allison set up a mini aquarium for her new pet. Visitors to the aquarium could see the large crayfish in some water with a rock. For a more enriched experience though, viewers could watch Allie poke the bucket and make him move an inch or two! By the late afternoon, the decaboda was returned to the wild, and set loose in the stream near BBSG to happily swim with his other crawdad friends.
