## Belle’s Castle

Several individuals have remarked over the past few days about how much the Blackwood Manor dining room currently resembles the palace ballroom from Beauty and the Beast. “It’s just like Belle’s Castle!” remarked Allison Blackwood upon her first entry of the newly painted room. Apparently, the electrician later unknowingly agreed with her. The walls appear to be periwinkle blue, white, and shimmery gold at first glance. But one will realize that the gold trim is actually just un-painted wood upon closer inspection. Other BBSG students have commented that the Manor’s foyer appears somewhat like a very very very mini version of the Palace of Versailles's entry hall. Those of you who have visited the new Blackwood Boarding School building may recognize the similarity, however one of the builders exclaimed,“I don’t know what they’re all talking about.” 

## Brewed to Perfection

“It was neat.” said Elsie Blackwood after attending the wedding of Ellie Faris and Walter Sanders. As some of you may recall, Blackwood Boarding hosted Ellie’s coffee-themed bridal shower a few weeks ago. The wedding also included a coffee bar of course. Flo B. was snapping away at the reception as usual (they may as well have hired her as a photographer.) And she made sure to record all the cousins dancing too. But, it was a delightful time, and BBSG welcomes Walter as our 5th cousin-in-law! 

## A Chilly Race

The annual Run For Justice took place this Saturday, and the participants ran their best even in the icy weather that day. Most of Team RGC ran the 5 and 10ks, but a few cheered on the runners with encouraging signs reading, “Hey you, nice legs!” and “STOP READING AND START RUNNING!”. Felicity Blackwood finished the 5k in 39 minutes, and Elsie came behind her in 40 minutes. They waited and waited and waited and waited until finally (after most of their team had already left) the rest of BBSG jogged to the finish line, with a swift time of one hour and five minutes! They may have been the very last racers, but chattering teeth and freezing lungs didn’t stop them.
