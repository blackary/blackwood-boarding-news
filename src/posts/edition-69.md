## More Animals on the Way 

Allison and Lottie Blackwood were thrilled this week to discover nine new kittens born at Grandma and Grandpa Faris’s home, some of which they will hopefully be able to take home to Blackwood Manor when they are old enough. “At first we were going to get some kitties from Miriam’s husband’s parents, but too many died, so they needed the ones that were left.” said Allison, “I’m so excited, and I named my kitty Muffin. She’s a tiger kitten! And we’re gonna get them right after the Blackwood Bash,” in mid-June. Some workers at the Manor have attempted to bring full-grown cats to slow down the rapidly growing mice population, but these attempts have been unsuccessful. Flo B. has been on the hunt for kittens ever since, so if all goes well these cats will survive to adulthood, and the felines will hunt to their bellies’ content on BBSG’s new school grounds. 

## A Courageous Quest Completed

Emory Blackwood demonstrated her unknown bravery and valor just days ago when she retrieved the postage out of 544 Angelic Lane’s mailbox. Why, you might ask, was this such a daring expedition to accomplish?  Inside what may have seemed like a harmless everyday mailbox, containing everyday junkmail and magazines, hiding in wait were two ginormous insects ready to pounce and attack the unsuspecting mail-checker! A huge, fuzzy spider and a long-legged cricket sat on top of the mail. Emory shrieked! But she reached her hand in slowly and pinched the corner of one of the letters, and gingerly pulled out every piece of mail one by one, until she had successfully retrieved our postage! “Wow, it must be a very slow week at the press to be talking about a couple of bugs in the mailbox.” -Emory B. 

## Opening Fishing Day of 2026

This week the first fishing party/bonfire of the year at Blackwood Manor took place, kicking off the summer at our new home. The burning committee nearly started a forest fire when the bonfire was lit, because the flames kept lighting the lawn and the branches of a nearby tree, but BBSG students quickly filled up buckets of water from Lake Felicity to save the day. After nearly burning down the neighborhood, everyone safely enjoyed fishing, disc-golfing, swimming, and even roasting cookie dough over the now non-life-threatening flames. As one BBSG student put it, “It was fun.” A lovely way to sum up the evening.
