## School Grounds on the Market

This week the school groundskeepers and students have been giving the old building its finishing touches, ready to sell. While the realtors give tours to potential buyers, all of BBSG has to get out of the house and go where? To quote principal Zachary, “Anywhere but here!” It has been tricky keeping everything in tip top shape ready for showings every day. Each morning, students immediately fold up their beds and hide away their blankets and pillows, and if they get anything out, it’s put right away after using. In the evenings students go to their rooms, lay a blanket and pillow on the floor and try to go to sleep again. Flo Blackwood says, “Well, I am loving living in a very clean home. BBSG students are doing a fabulous job of keeping things tidy. Emotions are all over the place for sure… I was also very touched that someone even wanted to buy my furniture.” The past few days have obviously been slightly stressful for Flo, but so far no explosions or sobbing or anything. 

## Love Is Brewing

On Saturday, BBSG was transformed into a café for our cousin Ellie’s coffee themed bridal shower! Blackwood Boarding’s iconic shower balloon arch was erected, this time in a dusty shade of blue. For the coffee bar there was blonde roast and brewed, with many flavorings, creamers, and other mix-ins and sweeteners. There were also bistro style hors d’oeuvres served; scones, muffins, yogurt parfaits, and lemon cake. “I don’t see how people can drink a whole cup of coffee with just a little bit of cream!” Elsie Blackwood commented. Well, even if the beverages didn’t taste incredible to some, the party was still a complete success, and the guests enjoyed themselves. 

## Second Cousin Reunion

Blackwood Boarding’s second cousins the Smiths who have been missionaries in South Sudan for the past few years were able to come over this week for lunch and afternoon on Sunday. The Spirydovich family, and the Nadia and A-A-Ron Smiths came over to join the party, but it wasn’t easy keeping all the relatives straight for some people! Zachary B. said, “It was very wonderful to see our long-lost cousins, but our girls were a little confused about which Smiths were our Lafayette first cousins, and which were our Indianapolis/South Sudan second cousins.” Despite the confusion, it was great to catch up with extended family.
