## New Cycling Pro

Just a few weeks ago, student Lottie Blackwood was learning the basics of riding her bike, but now she’s not only pedaling away with ease, she’s also pulling off some new tricks. This week she’s figured out how to lift her feet up and rest them above her handlebars while moving! In an interview, Lottie was asked, “How did you get so good at biking so fast?” she replied, “Cause…I’m so good at biking!”  Well, that’s the secret folks, if you want to be amazing on your bike, get good at it. 

## Interior Decorating–One Wall at a Time

Flo B. has been hard at work the past few days slapping up wallpaper in the dining room, library/guest room, and one of the upstairs bedrooms. While anyone over the age of 60 would strongly discourage wall-papering, Flo says that the new stuff is much more advanced and easier to take off. Trained by the only professional wall-paperer in Tippecanoe County, who is trying to retire, Flo B. and her friend Anna A. have been learning fast, and are hoping to be available to take on a few clients in the near future. 

## A Sad Goodbye to our Fluffy Friends

This week, Principal Zachary Blackwood finally insisted that the school chickens were too big for their small enclosure, and needed to graduate from their tub in the garage into a real chicken coop. It was hard for all the students, letting their darling chickies go away to live at Blackwood Manor. Now there will be no more taking them outside to play, or holding them on the couch while watching a movie, or washing out and refilling their water bowl. However, they are probably much happier with the larger space even though it isn’t quite finished. Principal Z still needs to attach the run so they can freely roam in the grass and sunshine, and eventually lay extra large, nutritious, and yummy eggs.
