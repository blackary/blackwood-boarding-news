## That Mad Time of Year Again

Like many of you, we have filled out our March Madness brackets this week, eagerly anticipating Purdue’s winning of the basketball championship! Student Lottie Blackwood would like it to be publicly known that principal Zachary made a monumental mistake on her bracket, saying that she voted for “the huskies” to win, but in reality she picked Purdue. Lottie was devastated when she found out, but after much emotional support and promises to spread the word that she did pick Purdue, she is on her way to recovery, but is still slightly disappointed. 

## Playground Conflict Resolution

When BBSG went to a public playground to swap Emory for her cousin Margaret for a sleepover, some of the cousins decided to pile into one hammock one by one until it was so full we could hardly move. We were laughing about this and continued to chat, until our neighbors, a group of girls in a hammock to our left, started accusing us of staring at them and whispering about them. It appeared they were being slightly hypocritical as far as that went. Then the accusers brought over their friend who they claimed took taekwondo classes, and could kick our heads off. “Bet you’re pretty impressed.” the girls stated. Right then, Flo Blackwood walked over to tell everyone it was time to leave. The taekwondo head-kicker-offer girl stepped up to her and said that “the one in the gray pants” was whispering about her and her friends, but then her friends disagreed and everyone started talking at once. Flo B. was too confused to keep track of anything, and ended the argument by apologising for whatever whoever did and took everyone to the car. 

## Grounds Keepers Hard at Work

This week the BBSG grounds keepers have been hard at work getting our current location in tip top shape, ready to sell in just a few days. Flo Blackwood explained “Well, it’s a little difficult with so many students romping and rolling around, but I think we’re making some progress, and we’ll be ready enough. Although I should also mention we have enlisted several students to help work on the school grounds and they have done an exceptional job especially on powerwashing. Although, I’m also struggling emotionally. It’s a little difficult with so many memories at the school grounds here.”
