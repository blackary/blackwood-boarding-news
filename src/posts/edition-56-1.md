## History History and more History

Since this is Blackwood Boarding’s last leg of the Repulsively Vitriolic trip, Flo B. has been cramming in as many historic sites as possible, so we don’t miss out on anything. This week, however, she has let everyone decide if they want to come, or stay home and read books. The majority of students have gone, and most have somewhat enjoyed visiting the places and learning plenty about history. When asked what she thought about these historical monuments Elsie Blackwood put it, “They’re historic.”

## Rock Climbing

At the last campground BBSG stayed at there was a fifty-foot rock climbing range that we were able to, well, climb! One student remarked, “It was nice, but it was really hot. And then I thought it was gonna rain, but then it didn’t and it was really annoying. I was super fast on the hardest ones you know.” 

## BBSG Anticipating Our Final Return!

As Long Joe number three shortens by the day, the Blackwood Boarding Girls anxiously await the end of our final trip and life in the RV for good! Soon the despised “Classy”, a blight on the landscape of the Blackwood family driveway will be withdrawn from the sight of our weary eyeballs at last! Even while the trip nears its completion, there are still a few days to go, and while everyone is eager to return to Indiana, these final hours may drive some of us to craziness. While chances of survival are slim, miracles can happen.
